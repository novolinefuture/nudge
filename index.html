NUDGE

An instrument loader for the Atari ST

The practice came first. Fifteen years of running Tunesmith live — running into its ceiling, building elaborate workarounds, running four STs side by side just to do what one should have been able to do. The instrument was always in there. There was no way to finish it.

The tools caught up. Not gradually — at a specific moment, reverse engineering and AI collaboration became capable of the sustained, precise work that 144KB of undocumented 68000 machine code requires. That's when NUDGE became possible.

The result loads into the ST at boot, transforms it, and disappears when the session ends. Not software. An instrument loader — the first product to use that name, because there was nothing worth naming until now.

Novo Line. Berlin. 2026.
